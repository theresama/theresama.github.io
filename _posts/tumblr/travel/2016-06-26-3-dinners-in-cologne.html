First night: Schnitzel 

First night in Germany - gotta order the schnitzel, whatever that is. I really didn’t know what schnitzel was when I ordered it; I thought it would be a sausage. When it arrived and looked nothing like a sausage, I thought it was a piece of thin deep fried dough. After I cut a piece, I finally realized that schnitzel is a thin piece of meat that’s been battered  & deep fried.

It was pretty good, but didn’t blow my mind. 

When asked what I wanted to drink with my meal, I made the mistake of asking for tap water. The server had a weird look for a moment but gave me the glass of tap water. When looking up German tipping etiquette, I found out that asking for tap water is a faux pas. Order the bottled still water. Whoops.

Second night: Himmel un Ääd

Learning from the first night, I ordered a beer. They come in cute little 200mL glasses! Now that’s my kind of beer. 

“Himmel un Ääd” is a traditional dish in Cologne: black pudding, mashed potatoes, onion, & applesauce. 

Anything with mashed potatoes is good by me, but it was the black pudding that was the highlight. I admit to have been a bit hesitant about it at first (black pudding is blood sausage), but forgot about any qualms after the first bite.

It came wrapped in crispy filo - a contrast to its smooth creaminess. The consistency was a bit creamier than the mashed potatoes, and they went together like two best friends. 

Heh, I actually thought the applesauce was a mushroom sauce at first - I’d only ever seen dessert applesauce. The slight tartness of the sauce offset the fatty flavour of the pudding & potatoes. 

Check out Reissdorf am Hahnentor for delicious local Cologne eats. 4 stars on Yelp with all German reviews - you know it’s legit :p.

Third night: German pork knuckle

I went back to Reissdorf am Hahnentor for dinner again, since the black pudding was so good. I was recommended the pork knuckle and was convinced.

IT’S HUGE. It could feed two people for dinner. The skin was crispy and sticky (I’d never had pork stick to my teeth) while the meat on the inside was so tender.
