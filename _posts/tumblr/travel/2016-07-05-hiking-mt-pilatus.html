Thurs. July 30 - Day 3 in Switzerland

Hiking the whole way from Alpnachstad  is labelled as “medium” difficulty for a duration of 4 - 4.5 hours up 5500ft of elevation, but I knew I wouldn’t be able to walk the next day if I did that. There’s one stop on the way at Amisgen at 4459ft which is quite a bit easier of a starting point.

Even knowing that I could hike the shorter distance, the steep elevation intimidated me and I almost wanted to just ride the rail all the way up. The forecast called for thunderstorms & I hadn’t packed hiking boots. I went up anyway, thanks to the nice ticket lady who convinced me saying it was easy enough.

The Numbers

Duration: 1.5 - 2 hours from Amisgen

Elevation: 640m/2100ft

Distance: 3.1km/1.92mi. I couldn’t find any posted information on the trail distance so I tracked it on my way using RunKeeper.

Bring: 1L of water & a snack. I did it in sneakers, but I’d recommend hiking boots.

The Journey

It started with the world’s steepest cogwheel rail (max gradient at 48%) from Alpnachstad up Amisgen at 4459ft. This was the scary part; I feel much better having the ground beneath my feet. 

The driver dropped me off at Amisgen and from there upwards, I was on my own. Except for the cows. The sound of the cowbells followed me most of the way. 

I’d read up a bit on the trail beforehand and expected cows along the way, but I thought they’d be in pastures or farms on the side. Nope - they are literally on the trail. I watched a cow poop in front of me - this is another reason for hiking boots.

Don’t forget to close the gate. Past the cows comes the rocks, and then the clearings in the valley. 

About an hour from the top, I could see the hotel atop Pilatus. That was the toughest part of the hike. The grassy & rocky path turned to small stones that slipped beneath my feet. I could hear the tourists at the top. 

It was super cloudy by the time I reached them - couldn’t see anything but white. A warm soup & coffee later - it’d cleared up enough to see the closest mountains, but nothing past that. On a clear day, you should be able to see everything until the horizon. 

Even on a cloudy day, it was worth the hike. I’d do it again just for the cows.
