Wed. July 29 - Day 2 in Switzerland: Making the most of a Swiss Rail Pass.

It was a long day. From Lucerne to Interlaken to Kleine Scheidegg up to Jungfraujoch, and all the way back again was over 9 hours on the train. No complaints when the scenery is this beautiful. The time whizzed by as fast as we did, through idyllic little towns that sat around blue lakes in green valleys beneath white snow capped mountains. 

Do these cows know how good they have it?

Several picturesque towns, many mountains, and a few train transfers later, I was at the “top of Europe”. Jungfraujoch is the highest railway station in Europe. There’s a scientific observatory up there, a viewing platform, some souvenir shops, tourist traps, and restaurants.

My favourite part: a snow fun section. Should I try sledding or snowboarding or skiing? I’d skied once in my life before - back in elementary school - and that was cross-country skiing. But yeah, why not? 

Thanks, ski-captain. 

This little molehill felt like a mountain. Pizza-ing as hard as I could to go down as slowly as I could - I survived.
