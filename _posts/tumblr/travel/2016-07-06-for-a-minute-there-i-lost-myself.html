Switzerland Days 5-7: Openair St. Gallen

This festival is the reason Switzerland was on my itinerary for this summer. I had to see Radiohead & this was the viable tour date. Seeing Radiohead was on my bucket list, right below “learn to make sushi” and above “publish a book”. They were also the last band on my concert bucket list. They were worth waiting for.

It rained almost the entire day, turning the ground into thick mud. I had on a t-shirt & a wind breaker. I bought a sweatshirt to keep warm & was still shivering as I stood in the crowd. No one really seemed to mind the conditions though. Almost everyone had a yellow raincoat & rubber boots. They were prepared. It rains a lot in Switzerland, one of them said to me. Festival attendees were mostly young groups of friends in their 20s, camping together & day drinking, enjoying the music on the side.

Radiohead wasn’t scheduled to take the stage until 10:45pm. After wandering around the festival grounds all day & scoping out the crowd dispersion after each set, I staked out a spot right in the front center, behind the girl at the barrier, at 8pm. 45 minutes of waiting, 75 minutes of Caribou - a Canadian electronic group - 45 more minutes of waiting - and finally, Radiohead. 

Yeah, they were perfect. Thom Yorke & I made eye contact. Ed O’Brien & I exchanged grins. Thom Yorke dancing warms my soul. How does Jonny Greenwood still look like he’s 17? 

They finished off their second encore set with Karma Police: “For a minute there, I lost myself. I lost myself”. sang the crowd. For a minute there, I lost myself. I lost myself. For a minute there, I lost myself. I lost myself…

It took me back to that first year of university. To sitting in the Hart House Arbour Room watching my jazz band conductor’s Radiohead cover band. I’d never listened to Radiohead. I didn’t know what the words were; the vocals just sounded like wailing. But it was hauntingly beautiful. I sat there listening & crying.

The next day, I went to the faculty of music library, took out all the Radiohead CDs, and ripped them into iTunes. Their music expressed my feelings better than I ever could. It still does. 

Second day for Mumford & Sons was tough. I was really starting to get nauseous from the smell of mud, and my whole body ached from the previous nights. This was my third time seeing them. They’re always phenomenal - full of feeling. But I wasn’t in the mood. I stood near the back. Then as soon as the first few guitar strums of Babel started, the exhaustion left and I danced my heart out. I still don’t know where that energy came from.
