Tues July 5

First day in Salzburg: I didn’t plan a thing. I let the sights, sounds, & scents guide my way. I walked past the Mirabell palace, through its gardens, by the cathedral, and wandered through the old town, obsessively taking in the colours & textures of each building. I love the pastel tones & brush strokes. The rock patterns & wood details.  

I stopped when through a doorway there drifted the unmistakable scent of fresh bread. It was the Stiftsbäckerei St. Peter - Salzburg’s oldest bakery from the 12th century, with its original wood-fired oven still in use for its fragrant natural sourdough loaves. I picked up a small fresh loaf for 1 Euro and munched on it while I wandered.

Little stalls with foods and trinkets are set up in the square.

I soon found myself at the funicular for the castle - Festung Hohensalzburg - which towers on the mountaintop over the old city.


Wed July 6

Second day, I had a plan: do the Mozart things. I had a late start to the day at around noon beginning with Café Tomaselli, where Mozart and his family used to frequent. Thanks to a handy Yelp review, I headed up to its roof patio for people watching over the square.

After a coffee, omelette, & pastry, I walked over to Mozart’s birhplace & childhood home. History fact: Mozart was born next door to an H&M.

Mozart’s older sister was also a talented pianist and composer; their father toured both of them around Europe as children when Mozart became old enough. But Nannerl didn’t start piano lessons until she was 7, while Wolfgang already knew how to play the violin and piano by 5. By the time she was old enough to marry, she had to take over household duties and the furtherest she ever went in her musical career was being a piano teacher. What if her talents had been nurtured starting from as young an age as her brother? What if gender roles hadn’t confined her to being a housewife? What if she was raised with the same expectations of being a prodigy pianist? I wish we could’ve known.

After the Mozart museum, I took a chocolate & cold brew break.

Had lunch/dinner at the oldest restaurant in Europe, dating back to 803! I had a traditional Austrian tafelspitz - boiled beef in a broth. The restaurant is definitely more a novelty for its setting. White tablecloths in a cave-like setting, with private rooms & views that would be a gorgeous backdrop for a wedding banquet. 

Stopped to enjoy a light & fruity glass of white on my way back.

That’s Salzburg.
